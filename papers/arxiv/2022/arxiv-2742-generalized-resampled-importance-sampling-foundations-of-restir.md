# Generalized Resampled Importance Sampling: Foundations of ReSTIR

## 摘要

As scenes become ever more complex and real-time applications embrace ray tracing, path sampling algorithms that maximize quality at low sample&nbsp;counts become vital. Recent resampling algorithms building on Talbot et al.’s [2005] resampled importance sampling (RIS) reuse paths spatiotemporally to render surprisingly complex light transport with a few samples per pixel.&nbsp; These reservoir-based spatiotemporal importance resamplers (ReSTIR) and their underlying RIS theory make various assumptions, including sample independence. But sample reuse introduces correlation, so ReSTIR-style iterative reuse loses most convergence guarantees that RIS theoretically provides.&nbsp;
