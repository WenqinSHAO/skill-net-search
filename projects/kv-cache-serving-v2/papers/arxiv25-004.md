# arxiv25-004 — KVShare

## Why it matters in this project

KVShare is a major paper for approximate cross-request reuse in multi-tenant settings.

## Architectural role

**Role**: approximate multi-tenant KV reuse architecture.

Its key architectural move is to allow cross-request sharing without requiring exact prefix identity, while explicitly managing quality risk.

## Benchmarking structure

### Scenario

- multi-tenant serving
- long-context requests with high TTFT pressure

### Workload shape

- cross-request reuse opportunities
- both prefill and decode deviations matter

### Metrics

- TTFT
- throughput
- accuracy relative to reuse and recompute baselines

## Comparison use

Compare KVShare with:

- LMCache
- SemShareKV
- DroidSpeak

The important axis is exact reuse versus approximate reuse with selective recomputation.

## Project takeaway

KVShare supports the idea that **reuse architectures can move from exact cache hits toward controlled approximation** when TTFT pressure is high enough.
