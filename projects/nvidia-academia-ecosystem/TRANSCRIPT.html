### 3. Key Research Themes Feeding Isaac

#### Theme A: Sim-to-Real Transfer

The single most important research thread. The progression is:

1. **DeXtreme** (2022) established domain randomization of physics parameters (mass, friction, damping, controller gains) across massive parallel environments as the core technique for crossing the reality gap.
2. **IndustReal** (2023) extended domain randomization to contact-rich assembly tasks where forces and torques matter, adding impedance control for compliant interaction.
3. **AutoMate** (2024) proved that the same approach generalizes across diverse part geometries.
4. **DextrAH-RGB** (2025) added visual randomization (lighting, camera pose, tiled rendering) and photorealistic rendering to bridge the visual sim-to-real gap.

The accumulated methodology is now a standard Isaac Lab workflow: define the task in Isaac Sim, randomize physics and visuals, train with PPO across thousands of parallel environments, and deploy through Isaac ROS with FoundationPose + impedance control.

#### Theme B: Dexterous Manipulation

From DeXtreme's single-hand cube rotation (2022) to DextrAH-RGB's general-purpose RGB grasping on Atlas (2025), the dexterity thread has progressed from proof-of-concept to product-grade:

- Fabric-Guided Policies (FGPs), introduced from the geometric fabrics research, provide a collision-avoidance and goal-reaching behavior layer that makes RL policies safer on real hardware.
- Teacher-student distillation has become standard: a privileged teacher policy (with full state access) is distilled into a student policy that sees only RGB or depth.
- DexMimicGen (2025) solved the bimanual data scarcity problem, generating 21K demonstrations from 60 human examples.

#### Theme C: Assembly and Contact-Rich Manipulation

Factory (2022) provided the simulation engine. IndustReal (2023) provided the sim-to-real methodology. AutoMate (2024) provided geometry generalization. SkillGen (2024) provided automated demonstration generation. Together they form a complete pipeline for contact-rich manufacturing tasks.
